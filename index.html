NEW SINGLE

Cascading

listen

This piece was foremost inspired by Thom Yorke's solo work - I really like the way he builds percussion loops and wanted to try my hand at layering a bunch of percussion and creating something out of it. A lot of the sounds are created "in the box", save for the percussion in the latter half, which I recorded myself using a hi-hat, a cowbell, and a clock. I created the original percussion layer and piano chord progression on the 30th of January, and spent the entire day on the 3rd of February adding the rest of the elements, with a little bit of touching up on the 5th. I've been so caught up in other projects since then that I nearly forgot about it, but I'm glad to finally share it. Not sure if I'll make more songs in this vein in the future, but I had a lot of fun experimenting with loops and recording to a grid with digital sounds while keeping it organic and imperfect.

It's not often I'll consider the "meaning" of an instrumental piece, but my dad came in and heard me working on the track and his comments about it changed my perspective on the whole project - and now to me it does tell a story, without even saying a word.

-Zayne Thompson // March 2025

So, who is Zayne Thompson?

Im a self-taught musician from the midwest US who has been playing drums since 2015, guitar since 2019, and bass & piano since 2020. Drums are still the instrument I feel most closely connected with, which is good considering my drum kit takes up the overwhelming majority of my room. I play my instruments daily - jamming out ideas, learning my favorite songs, and constantly working towards improvement.

I have been making recordings of myself as long as I can remember - using blank cassette tapes in a CD player, or little handheld voice recorders, or whatever means I had at my disposal. In 2020, I received a desktop computer and an audio interface for Christmas, and that prompted me to immediately begin experimenting with creating multitracked recordings and mixing them. Listening back to some of those early experiments is similar to looking in the mirror after getting a very regrettable haircut, but its a good thing Ive kept with it, even when I fall short of the consistent unreasonably high standards I set for myself.

Anyway, I make weird little songs that take inspiration from my ever-expanding music taste. I have spent hundreds of hours of tinkering around, whether recording cover songs or demoing original ideas, and have begun focusing foremost on improving my songwriting in the past year. I have a wealth of ideas scattered about within a maze of folders, aptly named SONGSSSSSSSSS; I just need to dig through them and piece the right ones together, I hope! I plan on continuing to make songs as fast as inspiration strikes me, and those worthy of an audience will debut here on my website and on my Bandcamp page, with streaming services soon to follow.

Outside the realm of audio, I am also an amateur artist and photographer - the photos on this site (aside from the portraits in this section) were all taken by me, usually while on drives or walks whilst listening to music. From time to time, Ill share a collection of photos or drawings if I think theyre interesting enough to warrant it. Speaking of music listening though, I listen to a lot of music. Im a big enthusiast of the album listening experience, and Im a chronic CD collector (my collection currently stands at nearly 500 CDs - Ive long since run out of shelf space)

Anyway, I hope that you find something Ive made interesting!

-Zayne Thompson // March 2025
